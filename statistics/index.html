Statistics Recipes

These are a series of reference notes and "how-to" guides on various concepts, formulas, and methods in statistics that I have found useful.

Mean, Standard Deviation, and Standard Error

The population mean and standard deviation refer to the underlying probability distribution and are given by \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) respectively.

If a set of measurements, \(x_i\), represents the entire population, then \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) may be calculated as follows:

\[\begin{align} \mu &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_i x_i, & \sigma^2 &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_i (x_i - \mu)^2. \end{align}\]

Otherwise, the data are only a sample of the total population, and the mean and standard deviation of the population may be estimated as follows:

\[\begin{align} \bar{x} &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_i x_i, & s^2 &= \frac{1}{N-1}\sum_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2. \end{align}\]

Note the change in notation. These two parameters, \(\bar{x}\) and \(s\) are referred ti as the sample mean and standard deviation.

Variance, Covariance, and Correlation

Variance:

\[\begin{align} \text{var}(x) &= \frac{1}{N-1}\sum_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2\\ &= s^2 \end{align}\]

Covariance:
The covariance is defined between two parameters, \(x\) and \(y\), of which we have \(N\) paired measurements.

\[ \text{cov}(x,y) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i(x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y}) \]

Correlation coefficient

The correlation coefficient, \(r_{xy}\), is not to be confused with (Pearson's) \(R^2\).

\[\begin{align} r_{xy} &= \frac{\text{cov}(x,y)}{s_x s_y}\\\\ &= \frac{\sum_i(x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sqrt{\sum_i(x_i - \bar{x})^2}\sqrt{\sum_i(y_i - \bar{y})^2}} \end{align}\]

Note that the factors of \(1/(N-1)\) cancel from the top and bottom of the latter equation.
